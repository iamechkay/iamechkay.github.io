FROM THE DESK OF PRESIDENT

Lets Ladakhify
(Providing a platform for like-minded youth, to collaborate on issues endemic to ladakh, in particular)

In a society, it is an idealist perception to anticipate that the educated will delineate the aspirations of the people. Rather, the reality flips to the other side of the coin, where the society itself looks down upon the 18 year old unfit for participating in planning of the society & as a result, students are restricted to the cozy cocoon of their rooms, surfing news channels, reading newspapers, expressing their frustration and rejecting the low level of politics, especially on social media and other forums.

What if Students marry with Social Issues?

Society is under-developed where there students are less inclusive. The students represent a mighty river, which if channelized in the right direction will herald a prosperous and towering sovciety, in the true sense. History is replete with example of charismatic leaders who addressed social issues during their student life and these leaders have brought revolutionary transformations in their societies which otherwise old-order couldn’t or wouldn’t prefer to. The historicity of our nation reveals the pivotal role that students played in over-throwing the British-yoke. E.g., the Young Bengal movement- a mighty storm of free radical thinkers that had the capacity to uproot everything before it- revolted against the existing religious and social structure of the society.

Student-Life is a vibrant and formative period where one can prepare to be a jack of all trades and inculcate better work culture. And this work-culture influences his thinking, his morality and his interaction, at the large. If one marries society in student life, he is more likely to be aware of issues & challenges that the nation and the outside world is facing. These issues develop in him a sense of thinking and responsibility to formulate better ideas to approach them. Further he learns how to interact with others and this debating skill develops him in a dominating, aggressive youth who realises the true battle of his life – selfless service to mankind and nation upbringing.

This marriage should be balanced where he may neither be a mute specatator to nepotism, communalism, corruption, etc, nor he may be channelised by unjust leaders to indulge in fractional or partisan politics. He must understand that if kept away from society, he is likely to grow into a lopsided personality and as such a burden on the society, dependent on others for his own survival (in the battle of social issues).

Case studies

A) All Manipur Students Union (AMSU) is a vibrant student union in North-Eastern part of India. The influence of this Union is so large that even the Central Govt takes it in notice. It has a large say in the society and as such possess the might to even go against general people’s verdict and achieve success where even the chunk of Government fails. Such must be the role of students, to keep check and balance on the system, and achieve success where the Government fails. The reason for why DU exempted North-East students from CHT ( Compulsory Hindi Test) but not the North-west counterparts, lies in the might and the demanding tendancy of such a student organisation, that knows how to marry with society.

B) Kargil Ladakh
Present status of Society:
•Highly monopolised old-order family business in an oligarchic setup for monetary gains, at large.
•Abysmally low accountability and nepotism at its zenith.
•No Women representatives, highly patriarchal society.

Due to the lack of determination and commitment among the old order, it took over 40 years to demand a tunnel, which is yet in a lacuna. Had new order substituted it, the youth would herald a new era back then, where they could achieve success at ease. (This potrays the lack of sense of guilt feeling)

Present status of students:
•Student Organisations efficient to achieve curriculum related goals but not societal.
•Lack of effucient platform for youth collaboration
